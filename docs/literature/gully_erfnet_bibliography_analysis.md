## Deep-dive: useful hints and confirmations (top 10)

For each of the top 10 references: short summary, **useful hints** we could adopt, and **confirmations** that our direction is aligned.

---

### 1. Gafurov, Yermolayev (2020) — Automatic Gully Detection: Neural Networks and Computer Vision
*Remote Sensing 12(11):1743. MDPI.*

**Summary:** U-Net (Keras) for gully detection from ultra-high-resolution satellite RGB; distinguishes active gullies from rills and balkas. European Russia; no DEM required. ~10% error rates (FN/FP); complex gully systems remain challenging.

**Useful hints:**
- **Confirms** gully detection from optical-only (no DEM) is feasible — aligns with Gully-ERFNet’s finding that low-res DEM did not help.
- U-Net as baseline for linear erosion features; our SatlasPretrain U-Net + loss improvements are on the right track.
- Expanding training data and addressing complex/branching structures are stated next steps — we can treat narrow/discontinuous lobes similarly (data + loss/architecture).

**Confirmations:**
- Deep learning for gully/linear erosion in RS is an established, comparable task.
- Optical imagery alone can suffice; DEM is optional and can hurt if low-res.

---

### 2. Chen et al. (2024) — Towards Accurate Mapping of Loess Waterworn Gully by Integrating Google Earth Imagery and DEM Using Deep Learning
*International Soil and Water Conservation Research 12(1):13–28.*

**Summary:** Loess waterworn gully mapping on Loess Plateau; 0.6 m Google Earth + 5 m DEM; slope-of-slope (abruptness) fed into improved Deep Learning framework. F1 90.5%, mIoU 85.3%, OA 92.3%. Beats RF, original DeepLabV3+, U-Net.

**Useful hints:**
- **DEM-derived slope-of-slope** (abruptness) helps when DEM resolution is sufficient (5 m vs 0.6 m imagery). We use slope; adding **curvature or slope-of-slope** could help if our DEM is good enough.
- Strong results with **improved DeepLabV3+** and topographic features — supports investing in both architecture (e.g. ASPP/PPM) and DEM-derived channels when resolution allows.
- Gully mapping with DL + topography in China is a direct domain parallel; same family of methods applies to lobes.

**Confirmations:**
- Combining optical + topographic (slope, slope-of-slope) in DL is validated for gully-like features.
- High F1/mIoU is achievable for linear erosion with the right loss, architecture, and features.

---

### 3. Liu et al. (2022) — Ephemeral Gully Recognition and Accuracy Evaluation Using Deep Learning in the Hilly and Gully Region of the Loess Plateau, China
*International Soil and Water Conservation Research 10(3):371–381.*

**Summary:** Deep learning for ephemeral gully recognition on Loess Plateau. DEM vertical accuracy noted as critical for performance. Flow-direction + edge detection variants; precision/recall in 60–90% range depending on site.

**Useful hints:**
- **DEM quality (vertical accuracy) matters more than raw resolution** for gully-related tasks — we should check DEM quality and consider downweighting or ablating DEM/slope if our DEM is coarse or noisy.
- Ephemeral vs permanent gully distinction; we have “lobe” vs background — similar imbalance and linear-structure challenges.
- Multi-site variability in precision/recall — our per-tile and per-basin analysis (e.g. val MAE by tile) is in line with good practice.

**Confirmations:**
- DL for gully recognition in China is well documented; our problem (linear structure, imbalance) is in the same class.
- Evaluating across sites/regions (we have tiles/basins) is appropriate.

---

### 4. Xu et al. (2023) — A Comparative Study of Loss Functions for Road Segmentation in Remotely Sensed Road Datasets
*International Journal of Applied Earth Observation and Geoinformation 116:103159.*

**Summary:** Compares loss functions for **road** segmentation in remote sensing — linear, thin structures, imbalance. GapLoss, cost-sensitive loss, NeighborLoss (spatial correlation); standard CE is insufficient for imbalance, continuity, and boundaries.

**Useful hints:**
- **Roads ≈ linear, thin, imbalanced** — directly comparable to lobes/gullies. Loss design for roads (GapLoss, cost-sensitive, spatial) is relevant.
- **NeighborLoss / spatial correlation** — we could explore a term that encourages spatial consistency (e.g. neighbor agreement) to reduce scattered predictions.
- Paper justifies **comparative loss experiments** — our ACL, UFL, Generalized Dice, boundary-aware tests are aligned with literature.

**Confirmations:**
- Loss choice is critical for linear-structure segmentation in RS; our focus on loss (ACL, UFL, etc.) is well placed.
- Class imbalance and boundary precision are shared challenges; addressing them via loss is standard.

---

### 5. Jadon (2020) — A Survey of Loss Functions for Semantic Segmentation
*IEEE CIBCB 2020. GitHub: shruti-jadon/Semantic-Segmentation-Loss-Functions.*

**Summary:** Survey of ~15 segmentation loss functions; introduces **log-cosh Dice loss**; compares on skewed/sparse segmentation. Some losses perform well across datasets as baselines; GitHub has Keras implementations.

**Useful hints:**
- **Log-cosh Dice** — smooth variant of Dice; could try as alternative to our Dice/IoU in combined or ACL-style loss.
- **GitHub repo** — many losses in one place; useful for implementing or porting (e.g. to PyTorch) additional options.
- Survey supports **systematic loss comparison** — we are doing that with combined, focal, ACL, UFL.

**Confirmations:**
- Dice-family and composite losses are standard for segmentation; our combined (IoU + Smooth L1) and planned ACL (Dice + Focal) are in line.
- Trying multiple losses and comparing val MAE / improvement over baseline is a sound approach.

### 6. Zhang et al. (2024) — Remote Sensing Image Segmentation of Gully Erosion in a Typical Black Soil Area in Northeast China Based on Improved DeepLabV3+
*Ecological Informatics 84:102929.*

**Summary:** **Same region and task** as Gully-ERFNet — gully erosion in Black Soil, Northeast China. Improved DeepLabV3+ for gully extraction; part of the same research ecosystem as our main paper.

**Useful hints:**
- **Same AOI and target** — any architecture/loss/data choice they validate (DeepLabV3+, improvements) is directly relevant.
- Replicates that **improved backbones and segmentation heads** (DeepLabV3+, PPM/ASPP-style) work for gully in this region — supports our use of SatlasPretrain U-Net and considering PPM/SE.
- Multiple groups working on gully DL in Northeast China — our lobe task is a natural extension; methods (loss, post-processing, DEM use) transfer.

**Confirmations:**
- Gully extraction in Northeast China with DL is an active, comparable line of work.
- Our focus on loss + architecture (and optional DEM) matches the regional literature.

### 7. Zhu et al. (2024) — Automatic Mapping of Gully from Satellite Images Using Asymmetric Non-Local LinkNet: A Case Study in Northeast China
*International Soil and Water Conservation Research 12(2):365–378.*

**Summary:** **Same region** — Northeast China. Asymmetric Non-Local LinkNet for gully mapping from satellite imagery; non-local attention for long-range context along linear structures.

**Useful hints:**
- **Non-local / long-range context** for linear features — we could consider non-local or self-attention modules (e.g. in decoder) to capture along-lobe context.
- LinkNet is lightweight; combined with non-local attention it targets efficiency and accuracy — similar to our lightweight + attention (SE/PPM) direction.
- Same region reinforces that **optical satellite + DL** is the standard setup; DEM is optional.

**Confirmations:**
- Northeast China gully mapping with DL is well represented; our lobe detection fits the same geographic and methodological niche.
- Attention (non-local, SE) for linear structures in RS is validated.

### 8. Wang et al. (2020) — Object-Based Mapping of Gullies Using Optical Images: A Case Study in the Black Soil Region, Northeast of China
*Remote Sensing 12(3):487. MDPI.*

**Summary:** **Same region** — Black Soil, Northeast China. Object-based (OBIA) mapping of gullies from optical images; pre-DL baseline; factors include topography, precipitation, soil, human activity.

**Useful hints:**
- **OBIA as baseline** — if we ever need a non-DL baseline beyond “predict 0”, OBIA or simple RF on tiles could be a reference.
- Confirms **optical imagery** is sufficient for gully mapping in this region; DL (Gully-ERFNet, Zhang 2024, Zhu 2024) builds on this.
- Factors (topography, etc.) align with our use of DEM/slope; multi-factor analysis could inform feature ablation.

**Confirmations:**
- Black Soil Northeast China is a standard study area for gully/erosion; our lobe work extends the same region/task family.
- Optical-first, DEM/topography as optional or complementary is consistent.

---

### 9. Shen et al. (2024) — A Foreground-Driven Fusion Network for Gully Erosion Extraction Utilizing UAV Orthoimages and Digital Surface Models
*IEEE Transactions on Geoscience and Remote Sensing 62:1–16.*

**Summary:** Fusion network for gully extraction from **UAV orthoimages + DSM** (high-res topography). Foreground-driven fusion to emphasize gully (foreground) vs background — addresses imbalance.

**Useful hints:**
- **Foreground-driven fusion** — explicitly models foreground (gully) vs background; we could explore foreground-weighted fusion or loss (we already use lobe weighting).
- **UAV + DSM** — when topography is high-res, fusion helps; Gully-ERFNet’s 12.5 m DEM did not help — resolution/quality of DEM is key.
- If we get high-res DEM/LiDAR later, fusion designs like this are a template.

**Confirmations:**
- Imbalance (foreground vs background) is commonly addressed by foreground emphasis in fusion or loss.
- High-res DEM/DSM can improve gully extraction; our coarse DEM may explain weak or negative DEM effect.

---

### 10. Yu, Yang, Chen (2018) — Semantic Segmentation for High Spatial Resolution Remote Sensing Images Based on Convolution Neural Network and Pyramid Pooling Module
*IEEE JSTARS 11(9):3252–3261.*

**Summary:** **Source of PPM** for high-res RS — CNN + Pyramid Pooling Module for multi-scale context; improves segmentation on high-res aerial/RS imagery. Gully-ERFNet uses this PPM idea.

**Useful hints:**
- **PPM in RS segmentation** — multi-scale pooling (Pyramid Pooling) is a proven add-on for RS; we listed SE+PPM in the backlog; this is the original RS-oriented PPM reference.
- High-res RS and multi-scale context — our 256×256 tiles could benefit from PPM between encoder and decoder.
- Direct architectural ingredient we can adopt (e.g. add PPM to our U-Net decoder).

**Confirmations:**
- PPM is a standard, low-risk improvement for RS segmentation.
- Our plan to try SE + PPM (from Gully-ERFNet and this ref) is well grounded.
